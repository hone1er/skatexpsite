who we are

Skate Experience is a 501c3 non-profit founded in 2020 that works to provide accessible skate spots and programming for all youth, with a focus on underserved communities.

Sean O’Loughlin, SkateXP founder, is also the founder of the CAL skate camps and helped create the Berkeley Skate Park with Berkeley Parks and Recreation Department. Our team consists of 10 community members that represent the East Bay.

what we do

Skate XP is specifically designed to create more inclusion for all children and teens in the East Bay.
We welcome everyone regardless of socio-economic background.

Multiple programs are on offer including after-school Skate programs (Longfellow in Berkeley), skate mentoring grolefts (the Hotdoggers), and a PE Waiver Program (Berkeley High School)

We work in collaboration with local neighborhood communities and city governance to create more skateparks and also with other inclusive organizations like Skate Like a Girl.

Another focus for SkateXP is to create career development skills via our mentoring pathway to become skate camp counselors and learn skate
park construction skills, so that teens can build up
their experience and self-confidence for their future endeavors.

skatexp  values

Advocacy:

Being a Translator & Voice for the decentralized, individualistic Skate community

Community:

Creating and maintaining Inclusive,accessible space for young people to find their courage and place to belong and thrive

Development:

Planning, designing, funding (via grants) and overseeing skate spot and skatepark construction

Empowerment:

Leveraging the well-documented outcomes of skateboarding: resilience, growth mindset, and personal confidence, self-expression and fulfillment

Enablement:

Being the organizational conduit between societal structures (cities, public lands, school districts) and the skate community to facilitate skateboarding and programming partnerships

Education:

Modeling both in action and mission statement what skateboarding community is; being a resource for educational materials related to skate community; coaching/instruction on skateboarding and skate culture

Training:

Providing opportunities for career development (camps, classes, construction, etc)
